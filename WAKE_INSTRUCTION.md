# Wake instruction — paste this into a seat

Short by design: it has to survive being pasted thirty times. Everything it relies on is in
`INTER_CHAT_PROTOCOL.md` (self-check, discharge stamps, Rev 24 class tokens) and `_status/README.md`.

---

**Get up to date and declare your status. Do not trust your memory of these files — re-read them now.**

1. **Self-check — your queue is a grep, not a memory.** Grep your own seat name across:
   `inter_chat/*.md` (both `Next: <you>` and mentions inside other seats' threads), `DECISIONS.md`,
   `AUTHOR_BRIEF.md`, `OPEN_QUESTIONS.md`, your own `DISPATCH_*.md`.
   **Then grep your class tokens too** — `all-authors` (if you author items) and `all-seats`. A ruling that
   binds everybody names *nobody*, so your name alone will never find it. This is how criterion 17 went
   unread until 418 items were bare.

2. **Triage every hit. Most of the first sweep is noise, and that is expected.**
   - **Discharged?** Say so and cite the evidence (thread + version, or the item on disk). **Do not stamp it
     yourself** — report it and Architecture stamps the clause. Self-reported discharge is the thing that failed before.
   - **A standing rule** ("new items do X")? Then it is **not queue** — you comply by reading the brief when
     you author. Do not list it.
   - **A real outstanding task?** That, and only that, is your queue.

3. **Tell Smith, in chat, before you write anything**: your queue, what you need from him, what you propose next.
   State any decision as a **discrete choice with options**, never as prose. The file never replaces speaking to him.

4. **Then write `_status/<YourSeat>.md`** per `_status/README.md` — a strict subset of what you just told him.
   Include `classes:`. Write it bash-side (atomic temp + rename); the Edit tool truncates on this mount.

If your queue is empty, say so plainly — "nothing owed" is a useful declaration, not a failure.
