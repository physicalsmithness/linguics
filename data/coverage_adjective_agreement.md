## Items I would like a reviewer to look at

These carry an `examiner_note` and warrant a second eye.

1. **adj_pos_pre_02** (Re-order: nuova / casa / una): I ban "una casa nuova" via `must_not_include`, but it is grammatical Italian (with a slightly different reading: brand-new vs different). Reviewer to decide whether to relax the ban. Same pattern applies to other pre_noun_canonical items where both orders are technically grammatical.

2. **adj_irr_antico_04** (Compare simpatici and antichi): Two markpoints, two buckets, deliberately contrasting stress rule vs antico's exception. The brief allows this; I want a sanity check on whether the pedagogical pay-off is worth the schema complexity.

3. **adj_co_ci_stress_04** (single markpoint covering a discrimination pair): I used a single markpoint with a single bucket because the MCQ choice is a pair. Could also be split into two markpoints; reviewer's call.

4. **adj_gia_ge_03** (saggie vs sagge): Graded credit at 0.8 for saggie. Other -gia_ge items give 1.0 to both grige and grigie. The bucket description licenses both for grigia but my reading is that saggia's variant is less established. Reviewer may want consistency.

5. **adj_buono_02** (buono studente): The architect's `examiner_note` field warns that "un bravo studente" is more common in practice. I keep the buono form so the allomorph is being tested but flag the alternative.

6. **trans_aa_en_it_10** ("a long-standing friend of mine"): The reference uses "un mio vecchio amico" with definite-article-style possessive, and an alternative without the possessive. The negative reference shows the wrong reading. Whether the marker should fire both pre_noun_canonical AND semantic_shift for a single phrase is an architecture question.

## What is NOT covered (gaps acknowledged)

- **Comparatives and superlatives.** `più alto`, `meno intelligente`, `il più simpatico`. These are agreement-relevant but live under a different top-level topic (comparison.* or similar). Out of scope.
- **Adjectives derived from nouns**: bambino-style nominalisation (l'italiano = the Italian language vs italiano = Italian, adj). Out of scope.
- **Demonstratives and possessives** as adjectives in their own right. Touched obliquely (mio, questo) but not bucketed here; lives in pronoun / determiner trees.
- **The full irregular stem-change set.** Three are bucketed (amico, greco, antico). Others (medico, monaco, porco, etc.) follow analogous patterns. Could be authored if the bucket is enriched.
- **Adverbs of degree affecting agreement.** poco/troppo are touched (`poche tovaglie`) but the cross-cutting "indefinite adjective" patterns are not.

### 8. "Re-order" prompts lost their canonical-position signal

When the prompt-rewriting pass stripped naming-the-rule language, several "Re-order" prompts also lost the word "canonically". The trade-off: a learner who orders the words grammatically but non-canonically gets marked wrong, with the explanation supposed to teach the canonical-position rule after the fact. This is a defensible design choice but not currently documented. Affected items are in `position.post_noun_default` and `position.pre_noun_canonical`.

### 9. Cosmetic severity-strip damage on the original 96 items

When `severity` was stripped via `replace_all`, the closing-brace formatting on the 96 items predating that change lost a line: the old shape had `"severity": "minor",` on its own line just before `"version": 1`, and the replace removed the severity line but did not re-tidy the surrounding whitespace, leaving an extra blank-line-then-comma pattern that looks slightly off in a diff viewer. The JSON parses cleanly and any parser handles it correctly, but the old items have a different visual rhythm to the newer ones. A one-shot prettifier (`python -m json.tool` round-trip, or equivalent) would normalise it.

### 10. Coverage doc was lagging the file state

Until this revision, the doc reported 183 grammar items and 28 translation items; the actual files contained 185 and 31 respectively. The discrepancy was the two top-up grammar items (`adj_grande_pre_05`, `adj_mezzo_05`) and the three semantic-shift translation items (`trans_aa_it_en_14`, `_15`, `_16`) added in a later pass. Lesson for future passes: update the coverage doc in the same turn as the file edits, not as a final pass.
